Dice

All dice rolled should be ten-sided dice (d10)

Dice Pools

When asked to roll (e.g. "roll Willpower"), you roll as many dice as you have dots (•) in the appropriate Trait

Many checks are rolled as a pool of dice from a pair of Traits (e.g. "roll Wits + Technology") which means you roll the dice for both traits and total the result

Wins and Losses

A result of 6 to 10 on a dice is a win

A roll with no wins is known as a Total Failure

Criticals

When rolling a pool, every pair of 0s you roll is a Critical and counts as an additional 2 wins

Tests

A roll's Difficulty is set by the Storyteller (or by an another character's opposed roll)

If you roll enough wins to meet or beat the Difficulty (or roll more wins than your opponent), your character succeeds

It may be important to pay attention to the number of wins by which you exceed the Difficulty of a roll; some tests, Disciplines, etc. grow in effectiveness according to a roll's margin of success

Hunger Dice

When your character suffers levels of Hunger, they exchange regular dice in their dice pool with Hunger Dice on a one-to-one basis

Hunger Dice do not replace dice rolled for Humanity, Rouse Checks, or Willpower

Hunger Dice function as regular dice except in certain circumstances:

Messy Critical

If a roll is a success and any Hunger Dice have rolled a 10, the result is a Messy Critical

Your character succeeds, but their Hunger compels them to act in a rash, destructive, or otherwise disadvantageous manner lacking foresight, subtlety, or self-control

Bestial Failure

If a roll is a failure and any Hunger Dice have rolled a 1, the result is a Bestial Failure

Your character fails and faces additional drawbacks in the form of a Compulsion due to their Hunger
