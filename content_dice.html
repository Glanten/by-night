Dice

All dice rolled should be ten-sided dice (d10)

Success and Failure

A result of 1 to 5 on a dice is a failure

A result of 6 to 0 on a dice is a success

Dice Pools

Attributes, Skills, Disciplines, and other Traits are measured in dots • each of which represents one dice in a character's dice pool

When asked to roll a pool of dice (e.g. "roll Willpower"), you roll as many dice as you have • in a Trait and count the number of successes

Other checks are rolled as dice pooled from multiple Traits (e.g. "roll Intelligence + Technology") which means you roll as many dice as you have in both Traits combined

Criticals

When rolling a pool, every pair of 0s you roll is a Critical Success and counts as an additional 2 successes

Tests

A roll is usually made as a test against a difficulty set by the Storyteller, or as an opposed roll against another character's roll

If you roll successes equal to or over the difficulty, or roll more successes than your opponent, you pass the test

It may be important to pay attention to the number of successes you get on a roll, as some tests, Disciplines, etc. grow in effectiveness the greater the margin of successes rolled

Hunger Dice

When your character suffers levels of Hunger, they exchange regular dice in their dice pool with Hunger Dice on a one-to-one basis

Hunger Dice do not replace dice rolled for Humanity, Rouse Checks, or Willpower

Hunger Dice function as regular dice except in certain circumstances

Messy Critical

If a roll is a success and any Hunger Dice have rolled a 0, the result is a Messy Critical

You pass the test, but your Hunger compels you to act in a rash, destructive, or otherwise disadvantageous manner lacking foresight, subtlety, or self-control

Bestial Failure

If a roll is a failure and any Hunger Dice have rolled a 1, the result is a Bestial Failure

You fail the test, and there is an additional penalty, in the form of a Compulsion, due to the Hunger within you
