Established vampires tend to pick up, and learn from, certain feeding habits.

Alleycat

Stalking, overpowering, and feeding from whomever you can in an opportunistic manner. This is little more than mugging mortals for their blood.

  • Pick a Speciality: Intimidation (Stickups) or Brawl (Grappling)
  • Gain 1 dot of Celerity or Potence
  • Lose 1 dot of Humanity
  • Gain 3 dots of criminal Contacts

Bagger

You buy, steal, or otherwise procure cold, bagged blood. Ventrue may not pick this Predator Type.

  • Pick a Speciality: Larceny (Lockpicking) or Streetwise (Black Market)
  • Gain 1 dot in Blood Sorcery (Tremere only) or Obfuscate
  • Gain the Feeding Merit: Iron Gullet •••
  • Gain the Enemy Flaw (••) someone you owe or have deeply upset

Blood Leech

You drink blood from other vampires, usually because you have a position of power, but sometimes through hunting or coercion. An extremely risky method of seeking the night's sustenance.

  • Pick a Speciality: Brawl (vampires) or Stealth (vampires)
  • Gain 1 dot of Celerity or Protean
  • Lose 1 dot of Humanity
  • Increase Blood Potency by 1
  • Gain a Flaw: Dark Secret (••) Diablerist or Shunned (••)
  • Gain the Flaw: Feeding (••) Prey Exclusion (mortals)

Cleaver

You covertly feed from your mortal family (or perhaps someone else's) and friends. Add the family to the Relationship Map. This almost always puts the Masquerade at risk.

  • Pick a Speciality: Persuasion (Gaslighting) or Subterfuge (Coverups)
  • Gain 1 dot of Dominate or Animalism
  • Gain the Flaw: Dark Secret (•) Cleaver
  • Gain the Advantage: Herd ••

Consensualist

You always get permission before you feed; this might be by posing as a charity blood drive, performing in some sort of "alternative" club, or by explaining the truth to your victims. The last method is especially risky as it is a direct breach of the Masquerade.

  • Pick a Speciality: Medicine (Phlebotomy) or Persuasion (Victims)
  • Gain 1 dot of Auspex or Fortitude
  • Gain 1 dot of Humanity
  • Gain the Flaw: Dark Secret (•) Masquerade Breacher
  • Gain the Flaw: Feeding (•) Prey Exclusion (non-consenting)

Extortionist

You force victims to bleed for you in exchange for security, surveillance, or a similar service.

  • Pick a Speciality: Intimidation (Coercion) or Larceny (Security)
  • Gain 1 dot of Dominate or Potence
  • Distribute 3 dots between the Advantages: Contacts and Resources
  • Gain the Flaw: Enemy (••) police or a vengeful escaped victim

Farmer

You feed exclusively from animals. Hunger gnaws at you constantly. Ventrue, and vampires with Blood Potency 3 or higher, may not pick this Predator Type.

  • Pick a Speciality: Animal Ken (specific animal) or Survival (Hunting)
  • Gain 1 dot of Animalism or Protean
  • Gain 1 dot of Humanity
  • Gain the Flaw: Feeding (••) Farmer

Graverobber

You feed from fresh corpses, mourners, cemetary visitors, and/or hospital patients.

  • Pick a Speciality: Occult (Grave Rituals) or Medicine (Cadavers)
  • Gain 1 dot of Fortitude or Oblivion
  • Gain the Feeding Merit: Iron Gullet •••
  • Gain 1 dot of the Advantage: Haven
  • Gain the Flaw: Herd (••) Obvious Predator

Grim Reaper

You feed on those about to die from other causes, constantly on the lookout for new victims in hospices, care homes, and shelters.

  • Pick a Speciality: Awareness (Death) or Larceny (Forgery)
  • Gain 1 dot of Auspex or Oblivion
  • Gain 1 dot an Advantage: Allies or Influence (medical community)
  • Gain 1 dot of Humanity
  • Gain the Flaw: Feeding (•) Prey Exclusion (healthy mortals)

Montero

Like beaters during a pheasant shoot, your retainers herd victims toward you for the kill.

  • Pick a Speciality: Leadership (Hunting Pack) or Stealth (Stakeout)
  • Gain 1 dot of Dominate or Obfuscate
  • Gain 2 dots of the Advantage: Retainers
  • Lose 1 dot of Humanity

Osiris

You feed from your church, cult, fanbase, or worshippers.

  • Pick a Speciality: Occult (specific tradition) or Performance (specific field)
  • Gain 1 dot of Blood Sorcery (Tremere only) or Presence
  • Spend 3 dots between the Advantages: Fame and Herd
  • Spend 2 dots between the Flaws: Enemy and Mythic

Pursuer

You study your victims, learning their patterns and routines until you find an opportunity for them to disappear without a trace.

  • Pick a Speciality: Investigation (Profiling) or Stealth (Shadowing)
  • Gain 1 dot of Animalism or Auspex
  • Gain the Feeding Merit: Bloodhound •
  • Gain 1 dot in the Advantage: Contacts (a morally flexible dweller of your hunting ground)
  • Lose 1 dot of Humanity

Sandman

You rely on stealth (or Disciplines) to feed from sleeping victims.

  • Pick a Speciality: Medicine (Anaesthetic) or Stealth (Break-In)
  • Gain 1 dot of Auspex or Obfuscate
  • Gain 1 dot of Resources

Scene Queen

Your victims adore you as a prominent member of a specific subculture, and you abuse this adoration to feed on them.

  • Pick a Speciality: Etiquette (specific scene) or Leadership (specific scene) or Streetwise (specific scene)
  • Gain 1 dot of Dominate or Potence
  • Gain the Advantage: Fame •
  • Gain the Advantage: Contact •
  • Gain a Flaw: Disliked (•) outside your subculture or Prey Exclusion (•) a different subculture to yours

Siren

You use sex (feigned or real) as a cover for feeding.

  • Pick a Speciality: Persuasion (Seduction) or Subterfuge (Seduction)
  • Gain 1 dot of Fortitude or Presence
  • Gain the Merit: Looks •• Beautiful
  • Gain the Flaw: Enemy (•) a spurned lover or jealous partner

Trapdoor

You lure prey to a specific location (amusement park ride, Turkish bath, fight club, haunted house) before you strike

  • Pick a Speciality: Persuasion (Marketing) or Stealth (Ambushes or Traps)
  • Gain 1 dot of Protean or Obfuscate
  • Gain 1 dot of the Advantage: Haven
  • Gain 1 dot of an Advantage: Retainers (a roper, major domo, etc.), or Herd (steady, regular visitors), or another dot in Haven
  • Gain a 1 dot Haven Flaw: Creepy or Haunted
