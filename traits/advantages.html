Advantages are divided into Merits (with Flaws being their flipsides) and Backgrounds

Merits and Flaws

Linguistics

Characters are assumed to be fluent and literate in their native language and the predominant language of the Chronicle's setting

Merit: Linguistics •
The character gains fluency and literacy in an additional language

Flaw: Illiterate (••)
The character cannot read or write; their Academics and Science Skills are caped at 1, and they cannot have a Speciality in them incorporating modern knowledge

Looks

Merit: Beautiful ••
+1 dice to appropriate Social dice pools

Flaw: Ugly (•)
-1 dice to relevant Social dice pools

Flaw: Repulsive (••)
-2 dice to relevant Social dice pools

Substance Use

Specify the substance, which must be present in a mortal's blood when you drink it for the purposes of this Advantage

Merit: High-Functioning Addict •
+1 dice to any one category of dice pool (i.e. Physical, Mental, or Social; specify when you take this Advantage) when the last person you fed on was on your drug

Flaw: Addiction (•)
-1 dice to all pools when the last person to fed from was not on your drug (except for actions that will immediately obtain your drug)

Flaw: Hopeless Addiction (••)
-2 dice to all pools when the last person to fed from was not on your drug (except for actions that will immediately obtain your drug)

Archaic

Only ancillae or older vampires may take either of these flaws

Flaw: Living in the Past (•)
You must take a seriously outdated Conviction (e.g. "Women are delicate flowers", "Lower classes exist only to serve") and suffer -1 dice to Social dice pools involving such archaic beliefs (except with vampires your age, or older, who may admire your old-fashioned ways)

Flaw: Archaic (••)
You cannot use computers, smart phones, or similarly advanced technology; your Technology Skill is permanently 0 and you may suffer additional -1 dice penalties involving modern technology

Bonding

So long as they do not contradict each other, you may combine Merits and Flaws from this Advantage

Merit: Unbondable •••••
You cannot be Blood Bound

Merit: Short Bond ••
The strength of Blood Bonds on you decreased by 1 every 2 weeks, instead of monthly

Merit: Bond Resistance •
For every level of this Merit (maximum 3), you gain +1 dice to resist a Blood Bond

Flaw: Bond Junkie (•)
You suffer a -1 dice penalty to defy a Blood Bond

Flaw: Long Bond (•)
The strength of Blood Bonds on you decrease by 1 every 3 months, instead of monthly

Flaw: Bondslave (••)
It only takes one taste of another's Vitae to Blood Bond you; the Storyteller may rule that you begin play as your sire's thrall

Feeding

Merit: Iron Gullet •••
You can feed on cold blood, rancid blood, or fractionated plasma (i.e. medically treated blood bags); none of these provide Resonances; Ventrue characters may not take this Merit

Merit: Bloodhound •
If you are close enough to smell a mortal, you can make a Difficulty 3 Resolve + Awareness test to detect the Resonance of their blood

Flaw: Prey Exclusion (•)
Define a class of prey (e.g. drug users, women, innocents, Hispanics) - feeding from this class, or witnessing other vampires feeding from them, incurs Stains on your Humanity

Flaw: Methuselah's Thirst (•)
Your Hunger can only be reduced to 0 by drinking the blood of a supernatural creature (unless their blood is treated by an alchemist, Thin-Bloods don't count); this minimum Hunger rises with your Blood Potency as it would for humans

Flaw: Organovore (••)
In addition to drinking blood, you must also eat human flesh and organs to slake Hunger; Resonance (if any) can only be acquired by devouring a human's heart

Flaw: Farmer (••)
You only feed on animal blood; you must spend 2 Willpower to drink human blood; Ventrue characters may not take this Flaw

Mythic

Merit: Eat Food ••
You can consume food and drink without effort; it requires no nourishment and must be expelled before resting for the day

Flaw: Folkloric Bane (•)
Pick a folkloric bane, you take Aggravated Damage from that bane (e.g. ultraviolet light, silver, holy water)

Flaw: Folkloric Block (•)
Pick a folkloric block, when faced with that block you must spend 1 Willpower not to shrink away from it (e.g. holy symbols, crossing running water, entering a home uninvited, white animals, garlic)

Flaw: Stigmata (•)
At Hunger 4, you begin to bleed from open wounds on your hands, feet, and forehead

Flaw: Stake Bait (••)
For you, a stake through the heart means Final Death

Thin-Blood Merits and Flaws

These merits and flaws are only for Thin-Blood characters; they do not have • values as each merit balances a flaw; they do not count towards a character's maximum dots in Advantages during character creation or when purchased by spending XP

Thin-Blood Merits

Anarch Comrades:
You may treat a specific Anarch coterie as a collective one-dot Anarch Mawla, so long as you stay in their good graces; write this coterie on the Relationship Map

Camarilla Contact:
You have the equivalent of a one-dot Camarilla Mawla who treats you poorly and constantly demands favours and information from you, promising membership in the Camarilla and a chance at becoming a full vampire if you perform admirably; write this contact on the Relationship Map

Catenating Blood:
You can Blood Bond and Embrace as a normal vampire; vampires created this way will be Thin-Bloods

Day Drinker:
Sunlight halves you Health tracker and removes your vampiric abilities (including Disciplines and Health benefits), but does not otherwise harm you; you still need to sleep sooner or later, and may suffer Impariment or torpor if you halve an already-damaged Health tracker

Discipline Affinity:
Gain one dot in a Discipline of your choice - treat this as an out-of-clan Discipline for purposes of spending XP on this Discipline; drinking blood with matching Resonance does not reward you with extra dots in this Discipline

Lifelike:
You have a heartbeat, can eat and drink normally, engage in sexual activities, and appear perfectly alive to everything except the most advanced medical inspections

Thin-Blood Alchemist:
Gain one dot and one formula in Thin-Blood Alchemy; you may purchase more dots and formulae through XP as normal

Vampiric Resilience:
You treat slashing and puncture wounds as Superficial Damage as a regular vampire would

Thin-Blood Flaws

Baby Teeth:
You cannot bite in order to feed, you must cut your victims or extract their blood using equipment such as a syringe

Bestial Temper:
You test for Frenzy as a normal vampire

Camarilla Brand:
A Camarilla Sheriff "mercifully" branded you as a Thin-Blood so that all know your nature

Clan Curse:
Pick a Clan Bane to suffer from as though you had Bane Severity 1; you may only take a Brujah or Gangrel Bane if you have the Bestial Temper Flaw, and may only take the Tremere Bane if you have the Catenating Blood Merit

Dead Flesh:
You suffer a -1 penalty to face-to-face Social tests with mortals, and medical examinations will reveal your body to be deceased; you cannot take this Flaw with the Likelife Merit

Mortal Frailty:
You cannot Rouse your Blood to heal, instead healing like a mortal; you cannot take this Flaw with the Vampiric Resilience Merit

Shunned by Anarchs:
You have somehow angered the Anarchs and now they would sooner throw you to the Camarilla than listen to your pleas; you cannot take this Flaw with the Anarch Comrades Merit

Vitae Dependency:
You cannot use Disciplines (including Thin-Blood Alchemy) unless you slake at least 1 Hunger each week by drinking another (non-Thin-Blood) vampire's blood; if you lose access to Disciplines this way, they return as soon as you slake at least 1 Hunger by drinking vampire blood

Backgrounds

Allies

Allies are mortals who help and support you - family, friends, or an organisation that owes you some sort of loyalty. A given Ally can help you once per story and may ask for help in return. Include your Ally or Allies on the Relationship Map.

The cost of Allies is a combination of their effectiveness and reliability; the maximum cost of a single Ally (or group of Allies) is (••••••).

Ally Effectiveness

• A weak mortal (no use in a violent situation)
•• An average mortal or group of tight-knit weak mortals
••• A gifted mortal or dangerous group of average mortals
•••• A deadly mortal, a gifted mortal with supernatural powers, or a well-armed group of gifted mortals

Ally Reliability

• They are available roughly half the time you call upon them
•• When you call upon them, they are available in 1d10 hours
••• When you call upon them, they appear as soon as possible

Enemies

Enemies are the opposite of Allies - mortals who appear at least once per story to oppose you. Write your Enemy or Enemies on the Relationship Map. Purchase Enemies as a Flaw with the following guidelines:

Enemy Effectiveness

(•) A gifted mortal or dangerous group of average mortals
(••) A deadly mortal, a gifted mortal with supernatural powers, or a well-armed group of gifted mortals

Contacts

A Contact is a mortal with access to information or materials that would otherwise be difficult for you to acquire. Include your Contacts on the Relationship Map

• Can do or acquire something cheap or common (Resources 1) e.g. a drug dealer, gossip columnist, or used car salesman
•• Can do or acquire something useful (Resources 2) e.g. veterinarian, small-time gun dealer, reporter
••• Can do or get something expensive or difficult (Resources 4) e.g. security systems expert, homocide detective, underworld fixer

Fame

You may use your Fame to improve Social tests (normally instead of another Trait) when appropriate ("don't you know I am?"); this should make tests against your fans, tests for hunting, and so forth, easier. Fame has its downsides, of course, so you may want to invest in a disguise.

By default, Fame applies to mortals, but you might instead be famous among vampires. Rare individuals might be famous among mortals and vampires.

Fame normally counts as a Merit, but may be purchased as a Flaw by picking Infamy or Dark Secret.

••••• National or global fame; a movie star, musical phenomenon, or former world leader

•••• Everyone who even vaguely cares about your field or social trends knows something about you

••• Most of the country, at the very least, know your name

•• Local celebrity, known by most of the city

• A select subculture knows and admires you

(•) Flaw: You owe a lot of money to the wrong people, or have made yourself generally odious; alternatively, your spouse, lover, or a close family member has Infamy (••)

(••) Flaw: You are a Cleaver, are known for several breaches of the Masquerade, have been Blood Hunted out of another city, or have grievously offended this domain's ruler

Infamy

Infamy is the Flaw version of Fame - you are well known for something horrible. At the very least, Social tests and reactions suffer by the value of the Flaw; at worst authorities may attempt to kill or capture you.

The one-dot version of Infamy also includes the equivalent of a one-dot Dark Secret.

Dark Secret

This Flaw costs one dot less than the equivalent Infamy, as it is largely unknown.

Influence

Whether through wealth, prestige, political office, blackmail, or supernatural influence, you have pull with the local mortal community. Influence applies most within one group or region of a city (e.g. organised crime, the media, the police, the church, the South Side, Manhattan Island).

You may use your Influence in place of another trait for relevant tests.

Merit: Dominant •••••
Lesser figures try to figure out what you want and do it first

Merit: Powerful ••••
Functionaries and footsoldiers obey you without good reason to do otherwise

Merit: Entrenched •••
Mortal factions and power-brokers hesitate to oppose you

Merit: Influential ••
People want to do you favours

Merit: Well-Connected •
You're guaranteed a respectful hearing

Flaw: Disliked (•)
-1 to Social tests involving any group in the city except your Allies, Contacts, and other explicitly loyal supporters

Flaw: Despised (••)
-2 to convince a neutral actor to politically support you or do you a favour; one group or region in the city always attempts to thwart you and your associates

Haven

Without an appropriate Background such as Influence, Resources, or Status, a character's default Haven is likely to be something like a room in an abandoned motel, a rented office space, a single self-storage unit, or a small apartment with blacked-out windows.

Points in Haven abstractly represent its size, security, and privacy; this increases the Difficulty third parties have finding, surveilling, or penetrating a Haven, and makes it easier for the resident vampire to notice danger whilst present.

Merit: Excellent Haven •••
Very large, secure, or private (e.g. an out-of-town compound, bank building, or decommissioned subway station)

Merit: Good Haven ••
Good size, security, or privacy (e.g. single-family home, row house, zoo enclosure, branch sewer tunnel)

Merit: Modest Haven •
Small, but more secure and private than default (e.g. basement apartment, crypt, locked warehouse storeroom)

Flaw: No Haven (•)
You must go to some effort (at least a basic test) to find a new resting place every morning

Haven Merits and Flaws

These values may stack with your Haven's base rating for certain tests and projects.

Merit: Hidden Armoury •
Each dot in this Merit adds a stand of arms: one pistol and one long firearm, with ammunition for both; their concealment and security is based on your Haven's base score

Merit: Cell •
A dedicated, locked space for two prisoners with an escape Difficulty 5 (not available in small Havens); each additional dot either allows you to double the number of potential prisoners (maximum 32, only in large Havens) or adds +1 to the escape Difficulty

Merit: Watchmen •
Each dot supplies 4 average mortal sentries and 1 gifted mortal boss, either as private security or criminal thugs; Haven watchmen may be conspicuous in certain areas

Merit: Laboratory •
Each dot supplies +1 to rolls related to one Science or Technology Speciality, or to Alchemy dice for Fixatio Thin-Blood Alchemy; (not available in small Havens)

Merit: Library •
Each dot supplies +1 to rolls for research in one Academics, Investigation, or Occult Speciality (small Havens are limited to one dot of this Merit)

Merit: Location •
+2 bonus to a relevant type of dice rolls, chosen when you pick this Merit (e.g. +2 Difficulty to enemies finding or penetrating your Haven, or +2 to hunting rolls)

Merit: Luxury •
+2 to Social tests when dealing with mortal guests (and perhaps other vampires); if you do not have at least Resources (•••), your decor was gained illegally

Merit: Postern •
Each dot provides +1 to evade or escape surveillance near your Haven thanks to a secret tunnel, sewer entrance, or other hidden passage

Merit: Security System •
Each dot gives +1 to resist (or alert you to) unauthorised entry into your Haven

Merit: Surgery •
+2 bonus to relevant rolls (typically Medicine) for surgical tests performed in your Haven (not available in small Havens)

Merit: Warding •
Each dot provides +1 to resist supernatural scrying, as well as other details worked out between you and the Storyteller; you may require Occult 3, or Blood Sorcery, to buy this Merit

Flaw: Creepy (•)
Social tests to put mortal guests at ease here suffer a -2 penalty, and neighbours may think it's the den of a serial killer or reclusive criminal (which it probably is)

Flaw: Haunted (•)
Your Haven features a supernatural manifestation that you do not control - it may be a ghost, cursed object, or dimensional portal; it provides at least a -1 penalty to the Haven's security, or other appropriate rolls, when invoked

Flaw: Compromised (••)
Your Haven was raided before, or it's on someone's watchlist; +2 to invaders and spies attempting to surveil or penetrate your Haven

Herd

A cultivated group of mortals from whom you may regularl feed without concern. They might perform basic services, but they are not as loyal or tightly-controlled as Retainers.

You might note your Herd on the Relationship Map

Each week, you may slake your Herd rating in Hunger without a roll; this benefit may be divided with others if you wish to share.

Merit: 31 to 60 Vessels •••••
Pick three Resonances every week; Herd maintenance and recruitment begins to endanger the Masquerade (e.g. large cult or sweatshop)

Merit: 16 to 30 Vessels ••••
Pick two Resonances every week; maintenance, cover-ups, and recruitment come up often (e.g. cult, sweatshop, or large brothel)

Merit: 7 to 15 Vessels •••
Pick two different Resonances every week (e.g. rugby team, swinger scene, dance class, night school class, night shift)

Merit: 4 to 7 Vessels ••
Pick one Resonance for half your Herd to share when you take this Merit (e.g. devoted club or goth scene, large cleaver family)

Merit: 1 to 3 Vessels •
One random Resonance (no Dyscrasias), you may change the Resonance once per month with a Difficulty 4 Manipulation + Insight test (e.g. graduate students, music pupils, cleaver family)

Flaw: Obvious Predator (••)
-2 penalty when hunting, except purely Physical expressions of stalking, chasing, and killing due to humans' instinctive fear and mistrust of you; -1 to Social tests intended to put mortals at ease; you may not maintain a Herd

Mask

A Mask is a vampire's human identity; by default characters have no need for a Mask or have a very basic one (e.g. a fake name and ID that will stand up to a routine traffic stop), but this identity will not stand up to a proper background check or investigation.

Merit: Excellent Mask ••
Your Mask can withstand probing from the national police (FBI, Scotland Yard, etc.); you may also purchase the Zeroed and Cobbler Merits

Merit: Good Mask •
Your Mask can withstand provincial or state-level checking and includes a birth certificate, bank account, credit history, etc.

Merit: Zeroed •
Someone has purged your records; you do not officially exist (this Merit requires a two-dot Mask)

Merit: Cobbler •
You can create or source Masks: creating a Mask takes 3 days per dot (and may expose you online if you're unlucky), sourcing a Mask only takes 1 day per dot but requires something in return (this Merit requires a two-dot Mask)

Flaw: Known Corpse (•)
People know you died recently and will react with shock and/or horror if they recognise you or look you up on a database

Flaw: Known Blankbody (••)
Your biometrics, name, history, known associates, and aliases appear in several intelligence agency databases - those in the know will easily be able to read between the lines and identify you as a vampire

Mawla

A fellow vampire, or small group of vampires (plural: Mawali, costs one more dot than a lone Mawla) who looks out for you, provides helpful advice, acts as a mentor, occasionally pushes or covers politically on your behalf, but expects reciprocation. Mawali do not fight your battles or call in favours on your behalf. Abusing a Mawla's helpfulness usually reduces dots in the Advantage.

Detail your Mawla or Mawali on the Relationship Map

The dots in the Malwa Advantage measure the power of the Mawla, as below:

• Neonate
•• Ancilla
••• Elder
•••• Primogen
••••• Prince or Baron

Adversary

The opposite of a Mawla is an Adversary: a vampire who wishes you (or more likely your sire, lineage, or mentor) harm; Adversaries range from a rankled (•) Elder or vengeful (••) Primogen to an angered (•••) Prince or cabal

Resources

The measure of your character's cash, gold, art, narcotics, investments, and other semi-liquid assets. Work with the Storyteller to detail the source of your wealth and income. Your Resources may be used in tests to acquire items, goods, property, or services.

Merit: Ultra Rich •••••
Many mansions and anything money can buy

Merit: Wealthy ••••
Mansion, helicopter or private jet, very specialised high-end equipment

Merit: Rich •••
Great condo or nice house, luxury items, high-end equipment for several people

Merit: Middle Class ••
Nice apartment or small house, several cars, high-end equipment

Merit: Porfolio Proletariat •
You live paycheck to paycheck; apartment, car, camping equipment

Flaw: Destitute (•)
You have no money and no home

Retainers

One or more steadfast servants or assistants; they may be ghouls or mortals you have subjected to Dominate and/or Presence so much that they are nearly incapable of independent action.

You must exert some sort of control over Retainers (e.g. salary, mesmerism, or a Blood Bond), and no Retainer is unfailingly loyal or flawlessly skilled.

••• Competent and independent enough to make lesser problems go away without supervision; likely began as a gifted mortal who is now a ghoul

•• A family servant, mortal lover, dominated thrall, or loyal ghoul

• A child, criminal low-life, or infatuated follower who does your bidding without a Blood Bond

Flaw: Stalkers (•)
You have a tendency to attract people who become a little too smitten with you; even if you manage to rid yourself of this hungry, lovesick, or desperate fool, another will soon appear

Status

You have a standing and reputation (earned or otherwise) within a specific local vampire community (usually the Anarchs, Camarilla, or Sabbat). Status in one city may apply in another, but at a penalty. You may use your Status to augment a Social dice pool, but overuse of it will draw negative attention.

Caitiff characters begin with the Suspect Flaw and may not purchase positive Status during character creation.

Merit: Luminary •••••

Merit: Powerful ••••
You hold office in your sect (e.g. Sheriff, Harpy, or Scourge)

Merit: Influential •••
You hold authority over part of the group (e.g. Elder, gang leader)

Merit: Respected ••
You have responsibilities now (e.g. Ancilla, Sweeper)

Merit: Known •
A properly introduced, welcomed nenoate, seen as an up-and-comer

Flaw: Shunned (••)
