These are the raw measure of your character's ability

Strength: Exertion of force by the muscles

Dexterity: Agility, grace, hand-eye coordination

Stamina: Toughness, resilience, endurance

Charisma: Charm, magnetism, strength of personality

Manipulation: Getting others to do what you want

Composure: Self-control, calm, cool-headedness

Intelligence: Memory, reasoning, intellect

Wits: Cleverness, intuition, spur of the moment decision making

Resolve: Focus, concentration, attention

Derived Attributes

Damage to Health or Willpower can be Superficial or Aggravated - most Superficial Damage is halved before it is applied to a character's tracker

Health

Typically harmed by injury and significant hardship; the lower it is, the closer you are to torpor and Final Death

A character who fills their Health tracker with Superficial or Aggravated Damage is Impaired (-2 to Physical tests)

Once a character's Health tracker is full of Aggravated Damage, they fall into torpor

Restoring Health

At the beginning of every session - mortals restore Health equal to their Stamina

Vampires may restore Health by Rousing the Blood, their healing rate depends on their Blood Potency; Aggravated Damage is much harder to recover from, requiring three Rouse Checks per point restored, and limited to a maximum of 1 point per night

Willpower

Confidence and mental stability

A character who fills their Willpower tracker with Superficial or Aggravated Damage is Impaired (-2 to Mental and Social tests)

Spending Willpower

Social Conflicts may reduce a character's Willpower, but it can also be spent in several beneficial ways:

  • Re-roll up to 3 dice (not Hunger Dice), except where the rules exclude the use of Willpower (e.g. rolling Humanity or a One-Roll Conflict)
  • Take control of your character for one turn while they are under the effect of a Frenzy or supernatural effect
  • Perform minute movements (twitch a finger, open eyelids) while staked
  • Ignore Health damage penalties (e.g. Impairment) for one turn

Restoring Willpower

  • At the beginning of every session - recover Superficial Willpower equal to the higher of your Compsure or Resolve (unless the last session ended on a cliffhanger, in the midst of an action scene, etc.)
  • Once per session - recover 1 Superficial Willpower when you definitively act to further your Desire
  • Recover 1 (or more) Superficial Willpower when you dramatically and convincingly play out a Messy Critical, Bestial Failure, Frenzy, or Compulsion (Storyteller's discretion)
  • Recover 1 (or more) Aggravated Willpower when you act to significantly benefit one of your Touchstones, or uphold one of your Convictions against your best interests (Storyteller's discretion)
  • At the end of a session - recover 1 Aggravated Willpower when you actively work toward your Ambition

Humanity: How far your character has degenerated into monstrosity - a character with 0 Humanity has completely succumbed to the Beast and can no longer be used as a playable character

Hunger: Part appetite, part lust, and part addiciton - a vampire with Hunger 0 is sated and satisfied; a vampire with Hunger 5 is ravenous and can think of barely anything except their next meal
