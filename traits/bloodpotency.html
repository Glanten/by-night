The Blush of Life

Cost: 1 Rouse Check per night

A vampire's body is functionally dead, signified by pale or ashen skin, cold flesh, lack of breath or heartbeat; the Blush of Life allows a vampire to recreate these lost characteristics and appear alive to all but the most intrusive medical screenings

Thin-Bloods do not need to use the Blush of Life for their body to appear alive

Blood Surge

Cost: 1 Rouse Check

Add bonus dice, depending on your Blood Potency, to an Attribute-based roll

For example, if you have Dexterity 3 and your Blood Potency grants you 2 bonus dice, you can make a Rouse Check to treat your Dexterity as 5 for a single test

Mending Damage

Superficial Damage to Health can be healed by making a Rouse Check (maximum once per turn)

Aggravated Damage to Health can be healed by making three Rouse Checks (maximum 1 point per night); at the earliest, this can be done on the nightfall after the damage was received

If healing Aggravated Damage would increase a vampire's Hunger to 5 or more, they fall into torpor rather than testing for Hunger Frenzy

Discipline Bonus

Dice pools to use or resist Disciplines benefit from a bonus equal to half the vampire's Blood Potency

Drinking blood with an Intense Temperament also grants 1 bonus dice to use or resist Disciplines with the appropriate Resonance

Discipline Re-Roll

You may re-roll your Rouse Check to activate a Discipline if it is at the appropriate level, or below, depending on your Blood Potency - this essentially gives you a better chance of activating a given Discipline without increasing your character's Hunger

Bane Severity

Each Clan has their own Bane, a drawback unique to members of that Clan that becomes more pronounced the greater the vampire's Blood Potency

Direct sunlight also inflicts Aggravated Health Damage to a vampire equal to their Bane Severity every turn; Thin-Bloods take 1 point of Superficial Health Damage per turn instead

Feeding Penalty

The more powerful a vampire becomes, the more restrictive their feeding becomes; blood from animals, or blood preserved in bags (without the use of medical anticoagulants etc.), cannot sustain older vampires

A vampire's Hunger cannot be reduced to 0 without completely draining (and thereby killing) a human victim; older vampires find they must do this to reduce their Hunger below higher and higher values as they grow in power
